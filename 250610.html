Support Trails and Win a Custom TransAM

  

Thanks to the generous support of Transition, SRAM, Shimano, Wolf Tooth Components, Schwalbe and OneUp Components, we are raffling off a brand new, size medium Transition TransAM. The chrome frame is tricked out with a choice selection of parts and built by Joe Brown at Methow Cycle and Sport. Raffle tickets are $20, or six for $100, and all funds go towards trail maintenance in the Methow. Tickets are available at Methow Cycle and Sport and at our Solstice Celebration (below). Grab 'em before we draw a winner on Saturday!

  
  

Celebrate the Solstice at Loup Loup

  

This Saturday, June 21, you’re invited to join fellow Methow riders at Loup Loup to celebrate the summer solstice, sample new trails and share burgers and brews. Group rides will leave the Loup Loup base area at 3 p.m. to explore Oso Peligroso, Ursus, Bear With Me, Telemark, Fozzie Bear Inventions or Buckaroo. Then stay for a BBQ and the TransAM raffle drawing at 5 p.m. (raffle tickets will be available). Kulshan Brewing will provide beverages. There’s no charge, but please RSVP by Thursday.

June Trail Notes

  

The last couple of weeks have brought both spring winds and summer heat, but riding continues to be sweet. At the Loup you'll find fields of purple lupine from top to bottom. Here’s the latest on what our trail crew has been working on:

  
  

Bear With Me Longer
The Bear With Me climbing trail at Loup Loup’s North Summit has been extended some 3000 feet and is now complete! Thanks to this extension you can now ride on singletrack from the base area to the top of Ursus. Look for the start at the far end of the parking lot, directly across from the exit of Ursus.

  
  

Middle Fork Beaver Creek Repaired
Our trail crew logged out and then repaired a significant washout on this backcountry classic. As temperatures warm on the valley floor, we highly recommend adding this trail to a Loup Loup or Bear Mountain loop. It’s a favorite descent for many and rides best before the cows arrive later this summer.

  
  

Sun Mountain Wind Event
Another windy day downed a significant number of trees, particularly on Climate Change and Thompson Ridge. The Evergreen crew jumped on it and the system should once again be clear. It might not seem like it, but Sun Mountain is a constant maintenance challenge. If you like riding and not climbing over trees, please consider donating to Evergreen so we can keep saws running and these favorite trails clear. Also, a huge shout out to Jeff Kastning who has volunteered to buck out a wide variety of trails. Thanks Jeff!

  

Building Continues at Loup Loup
We have finished the final layout of a new descending trail that will drop from Telemark to the base area. Look for ample rock features, steeper lines and a little air time at the end! We hope to complete this trail by the end of September. In the near term, we’ve completed a half mile of the Telemark to Oso traverse and the rest of it should be roughed in the coming week. At the Dig Days on June 18 and 28 we’ll continue work on this key link. Come join us!
