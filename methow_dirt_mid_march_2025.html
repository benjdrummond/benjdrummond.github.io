Stewarding our trails in a time of federal funding cuts and Methow Council openings

What the Forest Service Cuts Mean for Our Trails

  
  

I think it's fair to say we are in a challenging environment given the recent reduction of our federal work force. We have worked hard over the past fifteen years to develop and sustain a mutually beneficial and effective partnership with the Methow Valley Ranger District. Employees recently fired, not only carried out important work that directly benefitted the Methow Valley, they are our friends, neighbors and citizens. As a community that cares and heavily depends on recreation, we deserve better.

  

While we are currently heavily dependent on federal grants and funds, the Methow Chapter has access to enough resources in partnership with Evergreen Mountain Bike Alliance to continue our work this season. While we are directly affected by the federal cuts, Evergreen is working hard to realign resources so that we can keep our professional crews working and keep our projects moving forward. The real story though is much bigger than us. Our hearts go out to our most important partners who have worked or currently work for the United States Forest Service (USFS) and in particular, the Methow Valley Ranger District (MVRD). The Methow Valley is a tourism and recreation based economy and without the largest land manager operating at capacity, the Methow will be negatively affected on many levels. We all need to pitch in and help, show compassion and work tirelessly to find solutions to support and sustain recreation and all the other critical functions that the MVRD brings to the Methow.

  

We need to do our part to help and at the very least show some solidarity. That's what partners do and that means us. Let's focus on what we have control of and keep putting one foot in front of the other. We'll get through this and this year will be the easy one. Let's learn from this and prepare for the real challenges to come. Find time to enjoy riding your bike and focus on the things that matter. We’re all in this together.

  

— Joe Brown, President, Methow Chapter
