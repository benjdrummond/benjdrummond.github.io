Ritual MTB Film Tour Comes to Winthrop May 9

  

To celebrate the new season with our riding community, the Methow Chapter is bringing the Ritual Mountain Bike Film Tour to Winthrop. Join us at the Winthrop Barn on Friday, May 9 for a two-hour festival of films from around the world. Tickets are $10 for Evergreen members, when purchased in advance, and $15 for non-members or at the door. 

  

All proceeds from the evening will go towards trail building and maintenance in the Methow and help to alleviate recent funding cuts to our local USFS ranger district. Doors at The Barn open at 6 p.m. with a state-of-our trails update from Evergreen at 6:30 and films at 7. You'll also be able to connect with other Methow Valley trail and conservation organizations, grab a beer from Old Schoolhouse Brewery and enter your name in a raffle for exciting bicycle-related prizes.

Mud Season PSA

  

We get it. You're extremely excited to get your wheels back on the dirt. So are we! However, we ask that you be a good trail steward and be mindful of soil moisture and what kind of impact your tires might leave behind. If your tire digs in more than a half inch, please ride elsewhere and let the dirt dry out a little more. Trenches made it in the wet remain into the dry until one of our trail builders or volunteers completes tread repair work. If you do reach a wet spot, go straight on through rather than around which widens the trail. 

  

Good trail stewards:

  

1.

Let trails sufficiently dry out before riding
  

2.

Clear drains where puddles form on the trail
  

3.

Submit a report on the Trailforks app if maintenance is needed
  

4.

Lend a hand and volunteer for Dig Days!

Step it Up for Public Lands

  

This year a number of Methow Valley organizations are helping to steward the trails and common spaces we all enjoy. But they can't do it alone. If you value our public lands, grab your work gloves and sign up to lend at hand via the links below.

  

Methow Trails Collaborative Earth Week Work Parties
On April 23-26, MVTC will help the Methow Valley Ranger District prepare for the coming summer recreation season at USFS campgrounds and the Twisp River Trail.

  

Sunny M Barbwire Removal
The Methow Conservancy is working to remove miles of unnecessary fencing from Sunny M Ranch and along the Methow Community Trail.

  

MVTC Seeks Trailhead Ambassadors
WDFW and the Methow Valley Trails Collaborative are recruiting volunteer trailhead ambassadors for Lewis Butte and Pipestone Canyon, April through June.

  

Spring Cleaning at Homestream Park
On April 24, the Methow Valley Interpretive Center and the Methow Conservancy will host a workparty to get Homestream Park ready for the summer.

  

Mill Hill Trail Maintenance
Help the Methow Conservancy and Methow Valley Trails Collaborative install drainage and decommission social trails on the Ridge Trail, April 30 and May 2.
