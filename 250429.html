The Balsamroot is On and Sun Mountain is Open

  

Thanks to the quick work of Evergreen's professional trail crew and more than 100 hours of volunteer labor, we’re excited to report that the Sun Mountain trail network is cut out and ready to ride! Over the last two weeks the crew bucked more than 100 fallen trees, tuned up Coal Creek and brushed out the More Cowbell climbing trail. And yes, Upper and Lower Climate Change and the full Pete’s Dragon is open and riding beautifully. Later this week we’re planning to tackle downfall on one last snowy section on Thompson Ridge. Get out there and enjoy! And please report any issues that you encounter via Trailforks. It helps to include a photo and the approximate diameter of a tree so our crew can bring appropriate tools.

Don’t Miss the Ritual Film Tour in Winthrop May 9

  
  

The Methow Chapter is bringing the Ritual Mountain Bike Film Tour to Winthrop next Friday. Join us at the Winthrop Barn on May 9 for a two-hour festival of films from around the world that celebrate bikes, trails and the communities behind them. Tickets are $10 for Evergreen members, when purchased in advance, and $15 for non-members or at the door. All proceeds from the evening will go towards trail building and maintenance in the Methow and help to alleviate recent funding cuts to our local USFS ranger district. Doors at The Barn open at 6 p.m. with a state-of-our trails update from Evergreen at 6:30 and films at 7. Your ticket includes an entry for some bike-related door prizes and a free beer from OSB for those 21+. You'll also be able to enter your name to win something very special that begins with a T later this year… Bring a friend and see you there!

You Give Big, We Dig Big on May 7

  

It’s Give Big season and Evergreen is working to raise $120,000 for eight fully approved, dig-ready trail projects across the state. The Methow Chapter’s continued build out at Loup Loup (pictured above) will receive $15k if this goal is met. The North Summit plan contains over 40 miles of new construction and 20 miles of improvements to both multi-use and mountain bike-specific trails. Your financial support is critical to making this dream a reality. Give back to the trails you love and help build what’s next. Thank you!
